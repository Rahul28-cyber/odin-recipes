Odin Recipes

Malaiyo

Malaiyo, also known as Nimish or Daulat ki Chaat, is a delicate, frothy dessert that hails from the streets of Varanasi, India. Made by churning milk for hours and allowing it to absorb the morning dew on cold winter nights, Malaiyo is a seasonal delight available only during the winter months. The milk foam is gently flavored with saffron, cardamom, and sometimes garnished with slivers of almonds and pistachios, creating a cloud-like texture that melts in the mouth. It is light, airy, and subtly sweet — a perfect balance of flavor and elegance.

Chole Bhature

This North Indian classic pairs spicy, tangy chickpea curry with puffy, golden-fried bread. Chole is slow-cooked with aromatic spices, tomatoes, garlic, and onions, giving it deep flavor. The Bhature are soft inside with a crisp outside. Together, they create a rich, comforting meal often enjoyed with tangy pickles, onions, and a tall glass of chilled lassi — perfect for brunches or hearty lunches.

Misal Pav

Misal Pav is a spicy Maharashtrian street food that packs heat and texture in every bite. The base is made from sprouted moth beans cooked in fiery red gravy, then topped with crunchy farsan, chopped onions, fresh coriander, and a squeeze of lemon. Served with buttered pav, it’s flavorful, filling, and often eaten for breakfast or brunch. Each bite delivers crunch, spice, and tang all at once.

Appam with Stew

This South Indian delicacy combines fluffy, lacy rice pancakes (appams) with a creamy vegetable or chicken stew simmered in coconut milk. The stew is lightly spiced with ginger, curry leaves, and green chilies. The contrast of the crispy appam edges and soft centers with the warm, soothing stew creates a soulful dish. It’s nutritious, comforting, and deeply aromatic — perfect for breakfast or dinner.

Thai Basil Chicken

A fiery and fragrant Thai street food classic, Pad Krapow Gai is made with ground chicken stir-fried in garlic, chilies, and Thai basil leaves. The sauce is a bold mix of soy, oyster sauce, and fish sauce, delivering sweet, salty, and umami notes. Served with jasmine rice and topped with a crispy fried egg, it’s a quick, flavorful dish that packs an aromatic punch.

Kashmiri Dum Aloo

This rich, creamy curry hails from the beautiful valleys of Kashmir. Baby potatoes are deep-fried until golden and then slow-cooked in a luxurious yogurt-based gravy, flavored with fennel, dry ginger, and Kashmiri chili. The vibrant red color is striking, but the heat is subtle and warming. Best served with naan or steamed rice, it’s a comforting vegetarian delight with royal, aromatic flavors.

Golgappe

Golgappe, also known as pani puri or puchka, are a beloved Indian street food snack. They consist of crispy, hollow puris filled with spicy, tangy water, mashed potatoes, chickpeas, and tamarind chutney. Bursting with flavor, golgappe offer a perfect blend of sweet, sour, and spicy in every bite. Popular across India, each region adds its own twist. They're often eaten as a fun, interactive snack, challenging who can eat the most before the puris collapse or the spice hits!
